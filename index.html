Yang Liu (刘阳)

Email: liuy856#mail.sysu.edu.cn (Please change # to @)
Office: School of Computer Science and Engineering, Sun-Yat-sen University, Guangzhou, China

I am currently a Research Associate Professor at HCP Lab, School of Computer Science and Engineering, Sun Yat-sen University, working with Prof. Liang Lin. I obtained my Ph.D degree of Telecommunications and Information Systems in 2019 at Phoenix-eye Lab, School of Telecommunications Engineering, Xidian University, advised by Prof. Zhaoyang Lu. Before that, I received my B.S. degree in telecommunications engineering, School of Information Engineering, Chang’an University in 2014. I have authored and coauthored more than 15 papers in top-tier academic journals and conferences such as IEEE T-IP, IEEE T-II, IEEE T-CSVT, and IEEE SPL. I am the reviewer of CVPR, ICCV, ECCV, PRCV, IEEE TNNLS, IEEE TCYB, IEEE TMM, IEEE TCSVT, IEEE THMS, IEEE SPL, PR, and NSFC.
I am hosting several key national projects including NSFC, NSFC Guangdong, Postdoctoral Foundation, etc. I am the member of IEEE and IEEE Signal Processing Society. My research interests include:

  • Computer Vision: Video Analysis and Understanding, Human Action Recognition, Visual-Linguistic Reasoning, etc.
  • Machine Learning: Causal Reasoning, Transfer Learning, Domain Adaptation, Knowledge Distillation, etc.
  • Representation Learning: Deep Learning, Multi-modal Learning, Self-supervised Learning, Attention Modeling, Transformer, etc.
  • Urban Computing: Spatial-temporal Anomaly Detection, Traffic Elements Prediction, Data Mining, etc.
My representative papers are as follows:
  • Self-supervised Learning: TCGL: Temporal Contrastive Graph for Self-supervised Video Representation Learning, IEEE T-IP, 2022
  • Multi-sensor Learning: Semantics-aware Adaptive Knowledge Distillation for Sensor-to-Vision Action Recognition, IEEE T-IP, 2021
  • Multi-modality Learning: Deep Image-to-Video Adaptation and Fusion Networks for Action Recognition, IEEE T-IP, 2020
  • Multi-view Learning: Hierarchically Learned View-Invariant Representations for Cross View Action Recognition, IEEE T-CSVT, 2019
  • Heterogenous Representation Learning: Global Temporal Representation based CNNs for Infrared Action Recognition, IEEE SPL, 2018
